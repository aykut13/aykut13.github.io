The city, which lies at an elevation of 1,278 metres (4,193 ft)
in the broad valley of the Kızılırmak river,is a moderately-sized
trade center and industrial city, although the economy has traditionally
been based on agriculture.Rail repair shops and a thriving manufacturing
industry of rugs,bricks, cement, and cotton and woolen textiles form the
mainstays of the city's economy. The surrounding region is a cereal-producing
area with large deposits of iron ore which are worked at Divriği.
